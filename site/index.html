The real problem

You do not need more advice.

You need a system that remembers what you decided, spots contradictions, and brings the decision back when it is actually due.

01

Every conversation starts from zero

Strategy, pipeline, and clients live in different notes. AI answers your latest prompt, not the state of your business.

02

Important work ends as a paragraph

The brief sounds smart, but has no owner, date, or next move. A week later, nobody comes back to it.

03

You are the CEO and the bottleneck

You set the priority, chase the sale, calculate the margin, and judge your own plan. That is not a lack of ambition. It is a lack of structure.

You do not need AI that always agrees. You need a system that remembers, counts, and says no.

What makes it different

Conversations become company memory.

Founder OS does not pretend to be a magic autopilot. It builds continuity: shared state, clear ownership, operating cadence, and hard boundaries.

MEM

Memory that outlives the session

Goals, metrics, pipeline, clients, decisions, and drafts live in a local Markdown workspace. Tomorrow starts with business state, not another retelling.

OWN

One owner for every decision

The CFO does not rewrite the pipeline, and the Brand Editor does not change strategy. A write-time guard keeps every role in its lane.

RUN

A cadence that comes to you

A brief in the morning, pipeline review on Thursday, weekly review on Friday, and financials after month-end. Run it by hand or on a local schedule.

NO

Opinions, not business horoscopes

Every role has principles you may disagree with. A plan gets a verdict, a bet gets a kill condition, and “no time” gets a calendar audit.

One source of truth

Your business does not live in prompts.

It lives in simple files you can open, edit, archive, and version. Agents read the same state, but write only inside their own area.

Founder OS only knows what is recorded here. It does not automatically sync your calendar, CRM, inbox, or bank account.

  • Decisions remember not only what, but why — and what evidence would reverse them.
  • Drafts stay on disk; the version you actually sent teaches the system your real voice.
  • The queue has a cap, dates, and expiry — it does not grow into another wish list.
  • With multiple companies, each business keeps its own workspace and cadence.

Hard boundaries

AI advises. You decide.

No agent has a browser, shell, or tool that can reach beyond the workspace. That is a deliberate boundary, not a missing feature.

Human in controlalways on

Never sends. Never pays.

The system can prepare a proposal, follow-up, or rate-raise plan. You press the button, sign the document, and own the decision.

  • Messages and postsdraft only
  • Invoices and transfersno access
  • Legal and tax adviceescalate

An honest filter

This is for you — or it isn't.

Founder OS fits if…

  • you run a company of one or a small service business where every decision lands with you,
  • you use Claude Code and want every conversation to build shared business state,
  • you need a rhythm across pipeline, delivery, money, and focus,
  • you run multiple businesses and need to divide resources honestly.

Do not install it if…

  • you want an autonomous bot that sends messages and moves money for you,
  • you refuse to maintain any business state in files,
  • you need a full CRM with a shared dashboard for a large team,
  • you want an assistant that validates every decision without pushing back.

FAQ

Before you install.

Is Founder OS a separate app?

No. It is a Claude Code plugin. The agents, workflows, and rules install inside that environment, while your business state stays in a local Markdown workspace.

Are 13 agents running all the time?

No. They are specialized roles, not 13 autonomous processes running all day. A workflow invokes the relevant role; ten cadences can optionally run on a local schedule.

How long does setup take?

The plugin installs with two commands after adding its marketplace. Initial onboarding takes about 20 minutes and ends with a working workspace and your first brief.

Do I need to learn all 49 commands?

No. When you do not know what to use, ask the Chief of Staff. Routing is its job — it sends the question to the right agent.

Can it work in my language?

Yes. The technical section headings stay in English, but the workspace content and the agents' work can use your language. The system can also learn your style from real writing samples.

What if I run more than one business?

Each business gets its own workspace and cadences. The Portfolio Manager is the only role that looks across businesses, dividing time and cash without mixing operational state.

Where does Founder OS get its data?

Only from the Markdown workspace and context you provide in Claude Code. It does not automatically sync your calendar, CRM, inbox, or bank account. Files stay on your machine; Claude Code's own data-handling terms still apply to prompts and context sent through it.

How much does it cost?

Founder OS is free and MIT-licensed. It adds no account or subscription. Your existing Claude Code plan and usage are separate.

Does the system really run automatically?

After the optional /setup-cadences, local cron runs ten cadences while your machine and cron service are on. There is no cloud scheduler. You can invoke every cadence by hand.
